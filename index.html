About

I transitioned from a career in technical recruitment to pursue my passion for web development and become a software engineer. My previous role equipped me with a solid understanding of the tech industry and strong communication skills—assets that continue to support my growth in this new path. I'm currently expanding my technical foundation through Zuitt’s full-stack web development bootcamp, gaining hands-on experience in both front-end and back-end technologies. I'm eager to gain professional experience and specialize as a software engineer with a strong grasp of both front-end and back-end development, focused on building clean, user-centric digital solutions.

I decided to shift to web development because ever since I was in college, I always wanted to pursue programming, but I never got the chance to focus on it. As a technical recruiter, I conducted interviews for many different tech positions, which exposed me to various roles and technologies. Among all of them, web development stood out to me the most. I became especially interested in how web applications are built—from how they look on the front end to how they function behind the scenes. That curiosity pushed me to finally take the leap and start learning how to build things myself.

Currently, I have enrolled in the Zuitt Bootcamp so that I can gain hands-on experience in web development. Through this program, I’m learning how to build full-stack web applications, understand development workflows, and work on real-world projects. It’s helping me strengthen both my technical skills and problem-solving abilities, preparing me for a career as a full-stack developer.

My short-term goal is to become a confident, job-ready full-stack web developer. I'm actively building real-world projects, following best practices, and collaborating with other developers. I'm currently focused on understanding how core technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js, Java, and databases work.

My long-term goal is to work with a tech company where I can grow, contribute to real-world applications, and eventually take on more responsibility—like leading projects or mentoring junior developers. I also hope to build tools or platforms that solve real problems and have a positive impact on people’s lives.
